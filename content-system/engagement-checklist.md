## Comment Quality Rules

###  Do This

- Add a data point, example, or insight they didn't mention
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up question
- Share a relevant personal experience
- Respectfully challenge or extend their point

**Examples:**

> "Great breakdown. One thing I'd add: median is often better than mean for salary data because of extreme outliers at the top end. I showed this in a recent notebook: [link]"

> "This is underrated. I analyzed similar data and found that party size had almost no correlation with tip percentage — it was all about total bill."

> "Curious — did you control for seasonality in the A/B test? I've seen campaigns look successful because they ran during a holiday spike."

###  Don't Do This

- "Great post!" (zero value)
- "Thanks for sharing!" (invisible)
- "I agree!" (doesn't start conversation)
- Self-promotion without adding value first
- Generic emoji reactions

## Target Accounts to Follow & Engage With

### Data Analysts (your peers)
- People posting about SQL, Python, analytics projects
- People sharing portfolio work or learning journeys

### Recruiters (your opportunities)
- Data analytics recruiters
- Hiring managers at data-driven companies
- People posting job openings in analytics

### Educators (your community)
- Stats/data science educators
- People running LinkedIn newsletters about data
- Analytics course creators

### Students (your future community)
- MSc statistics students
- Data analytics bootcamp participants
- Career switchers into data

## Weekly Connection Strategy

- Send **5 connection requests per week** with personalized notes
- Note template: *"Hey [name], I've been following your posts about [topic]. I'm building a public statistics resource and would love to connect. — [Your name]"*
- Accept all incoming requests from people in data/analytics
