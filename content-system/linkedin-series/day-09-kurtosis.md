# Day 9: Kurtosis

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 9 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Kurtosis

Kurtosis tells us about the heaviness of the tails of a distribution.

In simple words:

It helps us understand whether extreme values are common or rare.

High kurtosis means more extreme values.

Low kurtosis means fewer extreme values.

Real-life example:

Stock market returns often show high kurtosis.

Most days have small changes.

But sometimes, the market moves sharply up or down.

These extreme movements create heavy tails.

Why does this matter?

Because extreme values affect risk, prediction, and decision-making.

Key takeaway:

Skewness tells us direction of imbalance.

Kurtosis tells us the chance of extreme values.
```
