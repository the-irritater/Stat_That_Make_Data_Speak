# Day 47: Simpson's Paradox

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 47 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Simpson's Paradox

Simpson's Paradox happens when a trend appears in separate groups but changes or reverses when groups are combined.

Sounds strange, but it happens in real data.

Real-life example:

A treatment may seem better for men and better for women separately.

But after combining both groups, the other treatment may appear better overall.

Why?

Because group sizes are different.

Data analytics use:

This matters in healthcare, education, business, marketing, and social research.

If we ignore groups, we may make the wrong conclusion.

Key takeaway:

Overall data can sometimes mislead.

Always check important subgroups before making decisions.
```
