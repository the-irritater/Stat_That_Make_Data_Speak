Day 36 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Missing Values

Missing values occur when some data points are not recorded.

They are usually shown as blank cells, NA, NULL, or NaN.

Real-life example:

In a student survey, some students may skip the income question.

Some may forget to enter age.

Some may leave optional questions blank.

Why does this matter?

Missing values can affect averages, percentages, models, and conclusions.

Common ways to handle missing values:

Remove the missing rows.
Fill with mean, median, or mode.
Use domain knowledge.
Use statistical imputation methods.

But do not blindly fill missing values.

First ask why the value is missing.

Key takeaway:

Missing values are not just empty cells.

They are signals that need investigation.
