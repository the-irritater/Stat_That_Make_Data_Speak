# Day 6: Mean, Median, and Mode

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 6 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Mean, Median, and Mode

Mean, median, and mode are measures of central tendency.

They tell us where the center of the data is.

Mean is the average.

Median is the middle value.

Mode is the most repeated value.

Example:

Marks of 5 students:

40, 50, 60, 70, 100

Mean = 64  
Median = 60  
Mode = no repeated value  

Real-life example:

Suppose most employees earn ₹30,000, but the CEO earns ₹10,00,000.

The mean salary becomes very high because of one extreme value.

In this case, median gives a better idea of the typical salary.

Key takeaway:

Mean is useful for balanced data.

Median is better when extreme values exist.

Mode is useful for finding the most common value.
```
