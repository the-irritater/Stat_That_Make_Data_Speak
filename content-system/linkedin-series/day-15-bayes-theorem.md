# Day 15: Bayes' Theorem

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 15 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Bayes' Theorem

Bayes’ Theorem helps us update probability when new evidence is available.

In simple words:

It helps us revise our belief after seeing new information.

Real-life example:

Suppose a medical test detects a disease.

But no test is perfect.

Bayes’ Theorem helps calculate the actual probability that a person has the disease given that the test result is positive.

A positive test does not always mean the person definitely has the disease.

Another example:

Email spam detection.

If an email contains words like “free”, “winner”, or “urgent”, Bayes’ Theorem helps estimate the probability that the email is spam.

Key takeaway:

Bayes’ Theorem is used when we want to update probability using evidence.
```
