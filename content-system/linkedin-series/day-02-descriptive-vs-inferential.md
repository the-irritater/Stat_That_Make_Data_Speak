# Day 2: Descriptive vs Inferential Statistics

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 2 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Descriptive vs Inferential Statistics

Statistics has two major branches:

Descriptive statistics and inferential statistics.

Descriptive statistics explains what has already happened.

Inferential statistics helps us make conclusions about a larger group using sample data.

Real-life example:

A company surveys 1,000 customers.

Descriptive statistics answers:

What is the average satisfaction score?

What percentage of customers are happy?

Which product received the most complaints?

Inferential statistics answers:

Can we say most customers in the entire market are satisfied?

Is the result strong enough to represent all customers?

Key takeaway:

Descriptive statistics summarizes data.

Inferential statistics helps us make decisions beyond the available data.
```
