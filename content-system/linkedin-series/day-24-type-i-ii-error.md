# Day 24: Type I and Type II Error

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 24 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Type I and Type II Error

In hypothesis testing, errors can happen.

Type I error means rejecting the null hypothesis when it is actually true.

In simple words:

False positive.

Type II error means failing to reject the null hypothesis when it is actually false.

In simple words:

False negative.

Real-life example:

Medical testing.

Type I error:

A healthy person is told they have a disease.

Type II error:

A sick person is told they do not have a disease.

Both errors are serious.

In business:

Type I error may make a company launch a product change that does not actually help.

Type II error may make a company ignore a change that actually works.

Key takeaway:

Statistics is not only about getting answers.

It is also about understanding the risk of wrong decisions.
```
