# Day 49: Bias in Data

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 49 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Bias in Data

Bias means the data or method systematically favors one outcome over another.

This is dangerous because biased data can produce confident but wrong conclusions.

Real-life example:

Suppose a college survey is shared only in one department.

The results may reflect that department's opinion, not the entire college.

That is bias.

Other examples:

Only surveying online users.
Ignoring non-respondents.
Using outdated records.
Collecting data from one region only.
Training AI models on unbalanced data.

Data analytics use:

Bias affects surveys, dashboards, statistical tests, machine learning, and business decisions.

Key takeaway:

Bad data does not become good just because we analyze it with advanced tools.
```
