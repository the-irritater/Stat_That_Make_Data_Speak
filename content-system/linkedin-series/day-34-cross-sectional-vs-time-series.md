# Day 34: Cross-Sectional vs Time Series Data

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 34 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Cross-Sectional vs Time Series Data

Cross-sectional data is collected at one point in time.

Time series data is collected over a period of time.

Real-life example:

If we collect income data from 1,000 people today, it is cross-sectional data.

If we record monthly sales from January to December, it is time series data.

Cross-sectional data helps compare individuals, companies, cities, or groups.

Time series data helps study trends, seasonality, and forecasting.

Data analytics use:

Customer survey data is usually cross-sectional.

Stock prices, website traffic, sales, rainfall, and inflation data are usually time series.

Key takeaway:

Cross-sectional data compares different units.

Time series data tracks change over time.
```
