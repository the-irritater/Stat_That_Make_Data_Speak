# Day 17: Normal Distribution

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 17 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Normal Distribution

Normal distribution is one of the most important distributions in statistics.

It has a bell-shaped curve.

In a normal distribution:

Most values are close to the average.

Very low and very high values are rare.

Real-life example:

Human height is often approximately normally distributed.

Most people have average height.

Very short and very tall people are less common.

Why is normal distribution important?

Because many statistical methods assume normality.

Examples:

t-test  
ANOVA  
Regression residual analysis  
Confidence intervals  

Key takeaway:

Normal distribution helps us model natural variation in many real-life situations.
```
