# Day 33: Primary vs Secondary Data

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 33 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Primary vs Secondary Data

Primary data is collected directly by the researcher.

Secondary data is already collected by someone else.

Real-life example:

If you create a Google Form and collect responses from students, that is primary data.

If you use government reports, Kaggle datasets, company records, or published research data, that is secondary data.

Primary data gives control over questions and sample design.

Secondary data saves time and cost.

But both have risks.

Primary data may suffer from poor sampling.

Secondary data may not perfectly match your research objective.

Data analytics use:

Before analyzing any dataset, ask:

Who collected this data?
Why was it collected?
How was it collected?

Key takeaway:

Data quality depends not only on numbers, but also on how the data was collected.
```
