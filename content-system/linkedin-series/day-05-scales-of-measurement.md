# Day 5: Scales of Measurement

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 5 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Scales of Measurement

There are four important scales of measurement:

Nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio.

Nominal means categories without order.

Example:

Blood group, city, gender, department.

Ordinal means categories with order.

Example:

Low, medium, high.

Interval has equal differences but no true zero.

Example:

Temperature in Celsius.

Ratio has equal differences and a true zero.

Example:

Age, income, height, weight, sales.

Real-life example:

In a customer survey:

Customer city = nominal  
Satisfaction level = ordinal  
Temperature rating = interval  
Monthly spending = ratio  

Key takeaway:

Scales of measurement help us choose the correct statistical technique.
```
