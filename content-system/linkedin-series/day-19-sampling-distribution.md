# Day 19: Sampling Distribution

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 19 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Sampling Distribution

A sampling distribution is the distribution of a statistic calculated from multiple samples.

Suppose we want to estimate the average height of students in a college.

We take one sample and calculate the mean.

Then we take another sample and calculate another mean.

If we repeat this many times, we get many sample means.

The distribution of these sample means is called the sampling distribution.

Why does this matter?

Because sample results change from sample to sample.

Sampling distribution helps us understand how much variation we can expect.

Key takeaway:

Sampling distribution helps us understand the reliability of sample-based conclusions.
```
