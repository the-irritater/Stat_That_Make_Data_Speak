# Day 14: Conditional Probability

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 14 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Conditional Probability

Conditional probability means the probability of an event happening given that another event has already happened.

Real-life example:

What is the probability that a student passes an exam given that the student studied more than 5 hours daily?

Normal probability asks:

What is the chance of passing?

Conditional probability asks:

What is the chance of passing given a condition?

Another example:

A company may ask:

What is the probability that a customer buys a product given that they clicked on an ad?

This is very useful in marketing analytics.

Key takeaway:

Conditional probability helps us update our understanding when new information is available.
```
