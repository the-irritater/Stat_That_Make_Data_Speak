# Day 18: Standard Normal Distribution

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 18 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Standard Normal Distribution

A standard normal distribution is a normal distribution with:

Mean = 0  
Standard deviation = 1  

It helps us compare values from different datasets.

The value used in standard normal distribution is called a z-score.

Real-life example:

Suppose two students score in different exams.

Student A scores 80 in Statistics.

Student B scores 75 in Mathematics.

Can we directly say Student A performed better?

Not always.

If the Statistics exam was easy and Mathematics was difficult, direct comparison is unfair.

Z-score helps compare both scores relative to their own class performance.

Key takeaway:

Standard normal distribution helps compare values from different scales.
```
