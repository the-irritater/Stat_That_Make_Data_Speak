# Day 12: Covariance

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 12 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Covariance

Covariance tells us whether two variables move together.

If covariance is positive, both variables tend to move in the same direction.

If covariance is negative, they move in opposite directions.

Real-life example:

Advertising budget and sales may have positive covariance.

When advertising increases, sales may also increase.

Price and demand may have negative covariance.

When price increases, demand may decrease.

Then why do we use correlation more often?

Because covariance has no fixed scale.

Correlation is standardized between -1 and +1.

Key takeaway:

Covariance shows direction.

Correlation shows direction and strength in a standardized way.
```
