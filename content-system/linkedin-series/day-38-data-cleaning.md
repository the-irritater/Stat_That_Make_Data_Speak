Day 38 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Data Cleaning

Data cleaning is the process of fixing problems in raw data before analysis.

Common cleaning steps include:

Removing duplicates.
Handling missing values.
Correcting spelling errors.
Fixing date formats.
Changing data types.
Removing irrelevant columns.
Standardizing categories.

Real-life example:

In a sales dataset, the same customer may appear twice because of duplicate entries.

If we do not remove duplicates, total sales or customer count may become incorrect.

Data analytics use:

Data cleaning improves accuracy, reliability, and trust in analysis.

A beautiful dashboard built on dirty data is still a bad dashboard.

Key takeaway:

Data cleaning is not boring work.

It is where reliable analysis begins.
