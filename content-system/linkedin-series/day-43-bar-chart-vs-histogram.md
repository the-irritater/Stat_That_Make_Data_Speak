# Day 43: Bar Chart vs Histogram

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 43 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Bar Chart vs Histogram

Bar charts and histograms look similar, but they are used for different types of data.

A bar chart is used for categorical data.

Example:

Product category, department, city, gender.

A histogram is used for numerical data.

Example:

Age, income, marks, height, screen time.

Real-life example:

If we show number of students by department, use a bar chart.

If we show distribution of student marks, use a histogram.

The mistake:

Many beginners use bar charts and histograms interchangeably.

That leads to wrong visualization.

Key takeaway:

Bar chart = categories.

Histogram = numerical distribution.
```
