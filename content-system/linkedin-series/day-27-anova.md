## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 27 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: ANOVA

ANOVA stands for Analysis of Variance.

It is used to compare means across more than two groups.

Real-life example:

A company wants to compare customer satisfaction across three cities:

Mumbai  
Pune  
Delhi  

If there are only two cities, we may use a t-test.

But for three or more groups, ANOVA is more suitable.

ANOVA checks whether at least one group mean is significantly different from the others.

Another example:

Comparing average exam scores across three teaching methods.

Key takeaway:

t-test compares two means.

ANOVA compares three or more means.
```
