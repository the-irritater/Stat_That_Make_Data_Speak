Day 57 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Homogeneity of Variance

Homogeneity of variance means different groups should have similar spread or variability.

Real-life example:

Suppose we compare exam marks across three teaching methods.

If one group has very stable marks and another group has highly scattered marks, the variances are different.

This can affect tests like ANOVA.

Data analytics use:

Homogeneity of variance is commonly checked before ANOVA and some t-tests.

Common tests include Levene's test and Bartlett's test.

But graphs also help.

Box plots can quickly show whether spreads look similar.

Key takeaway:

When comparing group means, do not only compare averages.

Also check whether the groups have similar variability.
