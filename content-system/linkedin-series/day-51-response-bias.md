# Day 51: Response Bias

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 51 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Response Bias

Response bias occurs when people do not answer questions truthfully or accurately.

This can happen for many reasons.

People may want to look good.

They may misunderstand the question.

They may choose socially acceptable answers.

They may rush through the form.

Real-life example:

If a survey asks, "Do you waste too much time on social media?"

Some people may underreport their screen time because they feel judged.

Data analytics use:

Response bias is common in surveys, feedback forms, interviews, and opinion polls.

Good question design can reduce it.

Key takeaway:

Survey data is not automatically truthful.

How we ask questions affects the answers we get.
```
