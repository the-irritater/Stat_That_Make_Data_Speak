# Day 11: Correlation

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 11 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Correlation

Correlation measures the relationship between two variables.

It tells us whether two variables move together.

Correlation ranges from -1 to +1.

Positive correlation means both variables move in the same direction.

Negative correlation means one increases while the other decreases.

Zero correlation means no linear relationship.

Real-life example:

Study hours and exam marks may have positive correlation.

As study hours increase, marks may also increase.

Screen time and sleep duration may have negative correlation.

As screen time increases, sleep duration may decrease.

But remember:

Correlation does not prove causation.

Key takeaway:

Correlation shows relationship.

It does not prove cause and effect.
```
