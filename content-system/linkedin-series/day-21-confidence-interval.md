# Day 21: Confidence Interval

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 21 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Confidence Interval

A confidence interval gives a range of likely values for a population parameter.

Instead of giving one fixed number, it gives a range.

Real-life example:

Suppose a survey estimates that average student screen time is 5.8 hours per day.

But this is based on a sample.

The actual average may be slightly different.

So we may say:

The 95% confidence interval is 5.4 to 6.2 hours.

This means the true average is likely to lie within this range.

Why is this useful?

Because sample estimates are never perfectly exact.

Confidence intervals show uncertainty.

Key takeaway:

A confidence interval is better than a single estimate because it shows the possible range of truth.
```
