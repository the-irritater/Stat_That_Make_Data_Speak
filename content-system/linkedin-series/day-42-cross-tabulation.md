# Day 42: Cross-Tabulation

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 42 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Cross-Tabulation

Cross-tabulation compares two categorical variables in a table.

Real-life example:

A college wants to compare department and preferred learning mode.

Rows: Department
Columns: Online, Offline, Hybrid

This helps answer:

Do statistics students prefer online learning more than economics students?

Does learning preference change by department?

Data analytics use:

Cross-tabulation is useful in survey analysis, market research, customer segmentation, and social science research.

It is also the base table used before applying the chi-square test.

Key takeaway:

Frequency table summarizes one categorical variable.

Cross-tabulation compares two categorical variables.
```
