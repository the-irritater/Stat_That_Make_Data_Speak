# Day 8: Skewness

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 8 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Skewness

Skewness tells us whether data is balanced or tilted to one side.

If data is symmetrical, skewness is close to zero.

If data has a long tail on the right side, it is positively skewed.

If data has a long tail on the left side, it is negatively skewed.

Real-life example:

Income data is usually positively skewed.

Why?

Because most people earn moderate income, but a few people earn extremely high income.

These high-income values stretch the data to the right.

Example:

₹20,000, ₹25,000, ₹30,000, ₹35,000, ₹5,00,000

The extreme value pulls the mean upward.

Key takeaway:

Skewness helps us understand the shape of data.

When data is highly skewed, median may be better than mean.
```
