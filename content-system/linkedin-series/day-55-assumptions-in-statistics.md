Day 55 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Assumptions in Statistics

Every statistical method has assumptions.

Assumptions are conditions that should be reasonably satisfied before using a method.

Real-life example:

Before using a t-test, we may check whether the data is approximately normal.

Before regression, we may check linearity, residuals, and multicollinearity.

Why does this matter?

Because using a test without checking assumptions can produce misleading results.

Data analytics use:

Assumptions help decide whether a statistical method is appropriate for the data.

Key takeaway:

Statistics is not just applying formulas.

It is checking whether the formula is suitable for the data.
