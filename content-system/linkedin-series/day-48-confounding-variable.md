# Day 48: Confounding Variable

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 48 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Confounding Variable

A confounding variable is a hidden factor that affects both the cause and the result.

It can make two variables look related even when the relationship is not direct.

Real-life example:

Ice cream sales and drowning cases may both increase together.

But ice cream does not cause drowning.

The hidden factor is summer.

Hot weather increases ice cream sales and swimming activity.

Data analytics use:

Confounding is important in research, regression, medical studies, marketing analysis, and social science.

Ignoring confounders can lead to wrong conclusions.

Key takeaway:

Before saying "X causes Y", check whether a third variable is influencing both.
```
