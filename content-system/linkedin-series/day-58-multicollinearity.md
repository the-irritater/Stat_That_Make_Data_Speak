## LinkedIn Post Copy

Day 58 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Multicollinearity

Multicollinearity happens when predictor variables in a regression model are highly related to each other.

Real-life example:

Suppose we predict house price using:

House area in square feet
Number of rooms

These two variables may be strongly related.

Bigger houses usually have more rooms.

This can create multicollinearity.

Why does this matter?

It becomes difficult to understand the individual effect of each predictor.

Data analytics use:

Multicollinearity is checked in multiple regression and machine learning feature analysis.

Common method:

Variance Inflation Factor, also called VIF.

Key takeaway:

When predictors are too similar, the model becomes harder to interpret.
