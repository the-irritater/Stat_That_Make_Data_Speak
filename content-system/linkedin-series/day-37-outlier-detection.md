# Day 37: Outlier Detection

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 37 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Outlier Detection

An outlier is a value that is unusually high or unusually low compared to the rest of the data.

Real-life example:

Monthly salaries:

₹25,000, ₹28,000, ₹30,000, ₹32,000, ₹5,00,000

The value ₹5,00,000 is an outlier.

But outliers are not always errors.

Sometimes they are important insights.

A very high transaction may be a fraud case.

A sudden sales spike may be due to a festival offer.

A very high score may indicate exceptional performance.

Data analytics use:

Outliers can affect mean, standard deviation, regression models, and dashboards.

Key takeaway:

Do not delete outliers blindly.

First check whether they are errors or meaningful signals.
```
