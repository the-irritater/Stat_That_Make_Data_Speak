## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 29 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Logistic Regression

Logistic regression is used when the output is categorical, usually yes/no.

Unlike linear regression, it does not predict a continuous value.

It predicts probability.

Real-life example:

A bank wants to predict whether a customer will default on a loan.

Output:

Default = Yes or No

Logistic regression estimates the probability of default.

Another example:

A hospital wants to predict whether a patient will be readmitted within 30 days.

Output:

Readmitted = Yes or No

Why is logistic regression important?

Because many business problems are classification problems.

Examples:

Customer churn  
Loan default  
Disease detection  
Spam detection  
Purchase prediction  

Key takeaway:

Linear regression predicts numbers.

Logistic regression predicts probabilities for categories.
```
