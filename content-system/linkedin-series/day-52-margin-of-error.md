# Day 52: Margin of Error

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 52 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Margin of Error

Margin of error tells us how much a sample result may differ from the true population value.

Real-life example:

A survey says 60% of students prefer online learning, with a margin of error of ±4%.

This means the true percentage may be around 56% to 64%.

Why does this happen?

Because we usually study a sample, not the full population.

Different samples may give slightly different results.

Data analytics use:

Margin of error is useful in surveys, election polls, customer research, and public opinion studies.

Key takeaway:

Survey results are estimates, not exact truth.

Margin of error shows the uncertainty around the estimate.
```
