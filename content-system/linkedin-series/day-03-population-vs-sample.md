# Day 3: Population vs Sample

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 3 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Population vs Sample

A population is the complete group we want to study.

A sample is a smaller selected part of that population.

Real-life example:

Suppose we want to study the average screen time of all college students in Mumbai.

All college students in Mumbai are the population.

But collecting data from every student is difficult.

So, we collect data from 500 students.

Those 500 students are the sample.

Why does this matter?

Because a bad sample gives a bad conclusion.

If we collect data only from computer science students, the result may not represent all college students.

Key takeaway:

A good sample should represent the population properly.

Bad sampling can make even good statistical methods useless.
```
