## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 32 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Structured vs Unstructured Data

Data can be structured or unstructured.

Structured data is organized in rows and columns.

Example:

Excel sheets, SQL tables, sales records, survey responses.

Unstructured data does not follow a fixed table format.

Example:

Images, videos, audio, emails, PDFs, social media comments, customer reviews.

Real-life example:

A company's sales table is structured data.

Customer feedback written in paragraph form is unstructured data.

Data analytics use:

Structured data is easier to analyze directly.

Unstructured data usually needs cleaning, text processing, image processing, or AI models before analysis.

Key takeaway:

Structured data is ready for analysis.

Unstructured data first needs to be converted into useful information.
```
