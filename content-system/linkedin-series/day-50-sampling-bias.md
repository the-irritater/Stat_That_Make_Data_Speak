# Day 50: Sampling Bias

## LinkedIn Post Copy
```text
Day 50 of Stats That Make Data Speak

Topic: Sampling Bias

Sampling bias occurs when the sample does not properly represent the population.

Real-life example:

Suppose we want to study all Mumbai University students.

But we collect responses only from our friends.

The sample is easy to collect, but it may not represent all students.

That is sampling bias.

Why does this matter?

Because statistical conclusions depend heavily on sample quality.

Even a large sample can be biased if it is collected badly.

Data analytics use:

Sampling bias affects surveys, polls, customer feedback, research studies, and market analysis.

Key takeaway:

A bigger sample is not always a better sample.

A representative sample is better than a convenient sample.
```
