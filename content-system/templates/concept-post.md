# Template: Concept Post

Use this for educational/theory posts. Goal: teach one concept clearly.

---

## Structure

### 1. Hook (first 2 lines — this is all people see before "see more")

```
[Bold claim / question / myth-busting statement]

[One-line follow-up that creates curiosity]
```

**Examples:**
- "Most people misuse the mean. Here's when the median tells the truth."
- "Your data has a shape. If you ignore it, your analysis is wrong."
- "Correlation doesn't mean causation. But here's what it DOES mean."

### 2. Body (3–5 bullet points or short paragraphs)

```
Here's the concept:

[Simple definition — no jargon]

Why it matters:

• [Point 1 — practical impact]
• [Point 2 — common mistake people make]
• [Point 3 — real example]
```

### 3. Real-World Analogy (optional but powerful)

```
Think of it like this:

[Relatable analogy that makes the concept click]
```

### 4. Key Takeaway (one bold line)

```
 [One sentence that summarizes the insight]
```

### 5. CTA + Visibility Loop

```
I'm building an open-source statistics resource with Python examples.

📂 Check it out: [GitHub link]

Follow for daily stats insights that make data speak.

#Statistics #DataAnalytics #Python #DataScience
```

## Example: Full Post

```
Most people misuse the mean. Here's when the median tells the truth.

If your data has outliers, the mean is lying to you.

Example:
5 employees earn: ₹30K, ₹32K, ₹35K, ₹38K, ₹500K

Mean salary: ₹127K
Median salary: ₹35K

Which one represents the "typical" employee?

The mean got pulled by one extreme value. The median stayed grounded.

 Use median when your data is skewed. Use mean when it's symmetric.

I'm breaking down statistics like this — with Python examples — in my open-source repo.

Follow for stats that actually make sense.
```
