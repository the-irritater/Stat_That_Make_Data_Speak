# Day 49: Bias in Data

*Advanced tools cannot fix bad data. Bias in, bias out.*

---

## Learning Objective

Understand how bias enters data at every stage — collection, processing, analysis — and why biased data produces biased decisions.

---

## The Concept

**Bias** means the data or method systematically favors one outcome over another. Unlike random error (which averages out), bias consistently pushes results in one direction.

### Types of bias in data:

| Bias Type | What Happens | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Selection bias** | Sample doesn't represent population | Surveying only urban areas |
| **Survivorship bias** | Only looking at what survived | Studying successful companies only |
| **Measurement bias** | Inaccurate data collection | Poorly calibrated equipment |
| **Confirmation bias** | Seeking data that supports beliefs | Ignoring contradicting evidence |
| **Historical bias** | Past data reflects past discrimination | Biased hiring data training AI |
| **Reporting bias** | Not all outcomes equally reported | Only publishing positive drug trials |

### Why it matters:

Biased data produces **confident but wrong** conclusions. A beautiful dashboard with biased data is still a bad dashboard.

---

## Real-World Example

A college survey shared only in the Statistics department shows 85% of students want more Python courses. But this doesn't represent the whole college — Arts students might prefer entirely different subjects. The survey has selection bias.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

> Bad data does not become good just because we analyze it with advanced tools. Identifying and addressing bias is a fundamental skill.
