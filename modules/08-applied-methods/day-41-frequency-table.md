# Day 41: Frequency Table

*The simplest way to make categorical data speak.*

---

## Learning Objective

Understand how frequency tables summarize categorical data and why they are the foundation of categorical analysis.

---

## The Concept

A **frequency table** counts how many times each category or value appears in a dataset.

| Learning Mode | Frequency |
|--------------|-----------|
| Online | 120 |
| Offline | 90 |
| Hybrid | 140 |

At a glance: Hybrid is most popular. This is the power of a frequency table.

### What frequency tables reveal:

- **Mode** — the most common category
- **Distribution** — how observations spread across categories
- **Imbalance** — whether some categories dominate
- **Rare categories** — values that might need grouping

### When to use:

Frequency tables work for any categorical variable — gender, department, city, product, payment mode, customer segment.

---

## Real-World Example

An e-commerce company counts payment methods:

| Payment Mode | Count | Percentage |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| UPI | 4,200 | 42% |
| Credit Card | 2,800 | 28% |
| Debit Card | 1,500 | 15% |
| Cash on Delivery | 1,000 | 10% |
| Net Banking | 500 | 5% |

Decision: Focus mobile app optimization on UPI since 42% of customers use it.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

> A frequency table is one of the simplest ways to make categorical data readable — and often reveals the insight you need.
