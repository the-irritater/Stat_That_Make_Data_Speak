# Day 52: Margin of Error

*Every survey result is an estimate. Margin of error tells you how good that estimate is.*

---

## Learning Objective

Understand what margin of error means, how sample size affects it, and why reporting it is essential for honest analysis.

---

## The Concept

**Margin of error** (MoE) quantifies the uncertainty in a sample estimate. It tells us the range within which the true population value likely falls.

### How it works:

If a survey reports: **60% prefer online learning, ±4%**

This means: the true population percentage is likely between **56% and 64%** (at 95% confidence).

### Key factors:

| Factor | Effect on MoE |
|--------|--------------|
| **Larger sample** | Smaller MoE (more precise) |
| **Smaller sample** | Larger MoE (less precise) |
| **Higher confidence level** | Larger MoE |
| **More variability in data** | Larger MoE |

### Rough formula:

MoE ≈ 1/√n for proportions (at 95% confidence)

- n = 100 → MoE ≈ 10%
- n = 400 → MoE ≈ 5%
- n = 1,600 → MoE ≈ 2.5%

Notice: to halve the MoE, you need 4× the sample size.

---

## Real-World Example

An election poll with 1,000 respondents shows Candidate A at 52%, MoE ±3%. The race is too close to call because the opponent at 48% is within the margin of error.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

> Survey results are estimates, not exact truth. Always report and check the margin of error — it shows how much the result could vary.
