---

## The Biggest Lessons

1. **Data quality matters more than method sophistication** — clean data with simple methods beats messy data with advanced methods.

2. **Correlation is not causation** — confounders, Simpson's paradox, and bias can all create false relationships.

3. **Statistical significance is not practical significance** — always ask if the effect is large enough to matter.

4. **Assumptions are not optional** — every method has conditions that should be checked.

5. **Residuals are your diagnostic tool** — they reveal whether your model is trustworthy.
