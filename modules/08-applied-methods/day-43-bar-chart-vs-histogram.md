# Day 43: Bar Chart vs Histogram

*They look the same. They are not. Using the wrong one means wrong visualization.*

---

## Learning Objective

Clearly distinguish between bar charts (categorical data) and histograms (numerical data), and choose the right one.

---

## The Concept

| Feature | Bar Chart | Histogram |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Data type** | Categorical | Numerical |
| **Bars** | Separated (gaps between bars) | Touching (no gaps) |
| **X-axis** | Categories | Number ranges (bins) |
| **Purpose** | Compare counts across categories | Show distribution of values |
| **Example** | Students per department | Distribution of student marks |

### The Common Mistake

Beginners often use bar charts and histograms interchangeably. This produces misleading visualizations because:

- Bar charts imply discrete categories
- Histograms imply continuous ranges

Plotting continuous data (income, age) as a bar chart hides the distribution shape.

---

## Real-World Example

- **Bar chart**: Number of customers in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore → compare cities
- **Histogram**: Distribution of customer ages → see if most customers are 25–35

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

> Bar chart = categories. Histogram = numerical distribution. Choosing the right chart prevents misleading conclusions.
