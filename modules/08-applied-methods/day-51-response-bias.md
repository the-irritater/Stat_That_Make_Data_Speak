# Day 51: Response Bias

*How you ask the question determines the answer you get.*

---

## Learning Objective

Understand how response bias affects survey data quality and how better question design can reduce it.

---

## The Concept

**Response bias** occurs when respondents do not answer questions accurately or truthfully.

### Types of response bias:

| Type | What Happens | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Social desirability** | People give "acceptable" answers | Under-reporting screen time |
| **Acquiescence** | People tend to agree | Saying "yes" to most questions |
| **Leading questions** | Question pushes toward an answer | "Don't you agree this is important?" |
| **Recall bias** | People misremember past events | "How many hours did you study last month?" |
| **Order effects** | First/last options chosen more often | Survey option order matters |

### How to reduce response bias:

1. Use **neutral wording** — avoid leading language
2. Offer **anonymous responses** — reduces social desirability
3. Use **specific time frames** — "this week" vs "in general"
4. **Randomize option order** — reduces position bias
5. **Pilot test** the survey — catch confusing questions early

---

## Real-World Example

Survey question: "Do you waste too much time on social media?"

This is a leading question. The word "waste" implies judgment. Respondents underreport their usage.

Better question: "How many hours per day do you spend on social media platforms?"

Same topic. Neutral framing. Better data.

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaway

> Survey data is not automatically truthful. How we ask questions affects the answers we get. Better question design = better data quality.
